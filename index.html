What brought me into software development?

My interest in software development began in middle school when I wrote my first BASIC program on my TI-83 plus calculator. Several years (and many calculator programs) later, I completed an introductory software development course in Java as part of my program for finishing my bachelors degree. Although the course was not very extensive, it provided me with my first experience writing code for a pc. This was limited to simple console applications and without an object-oriented focus, but it was sufficient for my needs at the time.

After finishing my bachelors, I began attending OSU for my graduate education in mathematics. Writing software in Java, or any other language for that matter, fell by the wayside at this point as I was intensely focused on studying. It wasn't until a few years later when I was doing research for my doctoral thesis that prompted me to begin writing code again. There were aspects to my research that relied on results that would not have been practical to calculate by hand, so I began learning GAP.
